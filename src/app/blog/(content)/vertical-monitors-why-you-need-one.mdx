## Explain the theory. State that we are "firm believers".

Mixture of Experts (MoE) is a wild and wonderful architecture that divides an AI model into multiple specialized sub-networks, each one an "expert" in a specific area. A "gating network" then decides which experts are best suited for the task at hand. It's like having a team of super-specialized AI brains working together, making the whole system more efficient and powerful.

And here at Vibe Coder, we're FIRM BELIEVERS in the power of MoE! We see it as a key to unlocking even greater AI capabilities in the future.

## HISTORY BLOCK

### Fun History Section

Did you know the Mixture of Experts concept was first introduced in a 1991 paper titled "Adaptive Mixture of Local Experts" by Robert Jacobs and Geoffrey Hinton? This ground-breaking paper proposed dividing tasks among smaller, specialized networks to reduce training times and computational requirements. Flash forward to today, and MoE is used in some of the largest deep-learning models, including Google's Switch Transformers and Mistral's Mixtral! Pretty groovy, right?
